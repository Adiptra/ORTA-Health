CAUSE OF
CHOLESTEROL

If the level of cholesterol in the blood exceeds normal levels, this condition is known as hypercholesterolemia or high cholesterol. High cholesterol conditions can increase the risk of serious disease. Cholesterol itself is a waxy fatty compound that is mostly produced in the liver and some is obtained from food.
Generally, heart attacks and strokes are diseases that lurk with high cholesterol caused by excessive cholesterol deposition in blood vessels.
Consuming foods with high cholesterol content or lack of exercise can also cause excess cholesterol. However, heredity can also be a trigger for cholesterol.

In
Addition

Medical conditions that can cause unhealthy cholesterol levels include:
a.Chronic kidney disease.
b. Diabetes.
c. HIV/AIDS.
d. Hypothyroidism.
e. Lupus.

Symptoms of

Cholesterol

High cholesterol usually does not cause any symptoms. In most cases, new symptoms are felt when high cholesterol leads to the formation of plaque in the arteries.

And
Then

Plaque can narrow the arteries so that less blood can pass through them. Plaque formation changes the arrangement of the lining of the arteries. When there is deposition on the artery walls due to excessive cholesterol levels, obstructions to blood flow in the heart, brain and other parts of the body can occur.

In
Addition

High cholesterol increases a person's risk for narrowing of the arteries or atherosclerosis, blood clots in certain parts of the body, minor strokes, strokes, to heart attacks.

Diagnose Of

Cholesterol

Examination of cholesterol levels in the blood should be done if you experience symptoms of being overweight, high blood pressure, have diabetes, or have other diseases that can increase cholesterol levels.

If there are close relatives who suffer from diseases caused by cholesterol, or there are close relatives who have a history of cardiovascular events at an early age, then a diagnosis of cholesterol levels is highly recommended.

Diagnosis of cholesterol is made based on the results of blood tests and physical examination of the symptoms experienced by sufferers. A family history check will also be carried out by the doctor. This aims to ascertain whether there are other medical conditions that cause high cholesterol levels in the blood.

Treatment Of

Cholesterol

The choice of drug or drug combination depends on a variety of factors, including personal risk factors, age, health and possible side effects of the drug. Common treatment options include :

01. STATINS

Options include atorvastatin, fluvastatin, lovastatin, pitavastatin, pravastatin, rosuvastatin and simvastatin.

02.
Cholesterol Absorption Inhibitors.

The drug ezetimibe, for example, can help reduce blood cholesterol by limiting the absorption of dietary cholesterol. Ezetimibe can be used with statin drugs.

03.

STATINS

BINDING RESIN.

For example the drugs cholestyramine, colesevelam and colestipol, can lower cholesterol indirectly by binding to bile acids.
