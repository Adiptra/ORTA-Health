WHAT DIABETES IS???

Diabetes is a chronic disease characterized by high blood sugar levels. Glucose is the main source of energy for human body cells. However, in diabetics, the body cannot use glucose.

The level of sugar (glucose) in the blood is controlled by the hormone insulin, which is produced by the pancreas. However, in diabetics, the pancreas is unable to produce insulin according to the body's needs. Without insulin, the body's cells cannot absorb and process glucose into energy.

Currently there are around 150 million people who have diabetes worldwide. Most sufferers only become aware of their diabetes after serious complications such as eye problems or kidney problems arise.

TYPE OF DIABETES

  • Type 1 Diabetes
    Type 1 diabetes occurs when the body's immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys the insulin-producing cells of the pancreas. This causes blood glucose levels to increase thereby triggering damage to the organs of the body.
    Type 1 diabetes is also known as autoimmune diabetes. The cause of type 1 diabetes is still not known with certainty. However, there are suspicions that this disease is related to genetic factors and environmental factors.

  • Type 2 Diabetes
    Type 2 diabetes is the most common type of diabetes, which is around 90–95%. Type 2 diabetes occurs when the body's cells become less sensitive to insulin so that the insulin they produce cannot be used properly. This condition is also known as insulin resistance.
    In addition to these two types of diabetes, there is a type of diabetes that usually occurs in pregnant women, namely gestational diabetes. This type of diabetes is caused by hormonal changes during pregnancy, but usually the patient's blood sugar will return to normal after delivery.

RISK FACTORS FOR TYPE I DIABETES

  • 4–7 years old or 10–14 years old
  • Have a family history of type 1 diabetes
  • Suffer from a disease caused by a viral infection
  • Suffering from an autoimmune disease
  • Experiencing injury to the pancreas

RISK FACTORS FOR TYPE II DIABETES

  • Over 45 years old
  • Have a family history of type 2 diabetes
  • Rarely do physical activity or exercise
  • Are overweight or obese
  • Suffer from prediabetes
  • Suffering from high cholesterol
  • Suffer from high blood pressure (hypertension)

RISKS

In general, diabetes is divided into two types, namely type 1 and type 2 diabetes. Type 1 diabetes occurs when the patient's immune system attacks and destroys the pancreatic cells that produce insulin. This is what causes an increase in blood glucose levels, resulting in damage to the organs of the body.

MORE RISK

Especially for women, pregnant women who suffer from gestational diabetes can more easily develop type 2 diabetes. In addition, women who have a history of polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS) are also more prone to developing type 2 diabetes.

SYMPTOMS OF DIABETES

Type 1 diabetes can develop rapidly within weeks or even days. Whereas in type 2 diabetes, many sufferers do not realize that they have had diabetes for years, because the symptoms tend to be non-specific.

SYMPTOMS for type 2 diabetes

  • Often feeling thirsty or very hungry
  • Frequent urination, especially at night
  • Weight loss for no apparent reason
  • Decreased muscle mass
  • Blurred vision
  • Urine contains ketones
  • The body gets tired and weak

In addition, there are several other symptoms that can also be experienced by diabetics, including :

  • Dry mouth
  • Itching of the skin or prurigo occurs
  • Erectile dysfunction or impotence
  • Burning, stiffness and pain in the legs
  • Reactive hypoglycemia, namely hypoglycemia that occurs several hours after eating due to excessive insulin production
  • Black patches around the neck, armpits, and groin (akantosis nigricans) which can be a sign of insulin resistance

Here are some tips that you can
do to prevent DIABETES

We hope these tips can help you

1. Exercise Routine

How to prevent diabetes through regular exercise can increase insulin production and the sensitivity of body cells to insulin. That way, blood sugar levels will be controlled properly, so you can avoid diabetes.
In addition, exercise also helps burn calories to produce energy and store excess glucose as energy reserves in the form of muscle protein as a reserve so that it does not accumulate in the blood.
Do at least 150 minutes of exercise each week, divided into 5 sessions or days, 30 minutes per session.

2. Adopt a Healthy Diet

How to prevent diabetes from an early age must also be accompanied by the adoption of a healthy diet. Try to reduce fast food, high sugar and fat. Start choosing foods with high nutritional content, such as protein, fiber and vitamins.
In addition, pay attention to food portions to reduce calorie intake in the body. Where these calories can cause weight gain, and if they are excessive, the risk of diabetes is higher.
Apart from paying attention to food portions, don't ignore the meal schedule either. A healthy diet is supported by eating regularly with sufficient portions.

3. Diligent Drinking Water

How to prevent diabetes at a young age can also be started by avoiding sweet drinks.
This refers to the risk of type 2 diabetes and autoimmune diabetes which can affect adults due to excessive consumption of sweet drinks.
To overcome this, drink water. When compared to drinks containing sugar, water can improve body hydration better and reduce sugar levels in the body. So it is beneficial for kidney function and also body cells.
In order to drink water regularly, you can feel it by bringing a bottle when you want to go out. In addition, also provide plain water on the desk or beside the bed to make it easier to reach
